## Task 14: Incident Response and Security Reporting

**Overview:** When an incident occurs, a structured response process minimizes damage and produces clear documentation.

**Why it matters:** In the middle of a real incident, panic and improvisation cause more damage than the incident itself — having a rehearsed lifecycle (Preparation → Identification → Containment → Eradication → Recovery → Lessons Learned) means the team knows exactly what to do next instead of debating it live. Clear, factual reporting afterward is just as important: it's what allows an organization to actually learn from the incident, satisfy legal/compliance obligations, and avoid repeating the same mistake.

**Key Concepts:**
- Incident Response lifecycle: Preparation → Identification → Containment → Eradication → Recovery → Lessons Learned
- Chain of custody for evidence
- Writing a clear, factual incident report

**Report Structure Example:**
```text
1. Executive Summary
2. Timeline of Events
3. Systems/Data Affected
4. Root Cause
5. Actions Taken (Containment/Recovery)
6. Recommendations to Prevent Recurrence
```

**Note:** Reports should be factual, timestamped, and free of speculation — state what is confirmed vs what is still under investigation.
